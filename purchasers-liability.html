Purchaser's liability

Purchasers acquiring certain property from non-residents of Canada

If we do not issue a Certificate of Compliance (Form T2064 or Form T2068) and the property is not excluded property, you as the purchaser may be liable to pay a specific tax amount. This tax arises from the vendor selling the property.

In this situation, you have the right to withhold a portion of the purchase price to cover this tax.

Withholding amounts

You can withhold the following percentages from the proceeds, minus any certificate limit:

  • 25% for most property types
  • 50% for certain specific types of property

Corporate shares and withholding tax

If you have corporate shares that do not get most of their value from Canadian real or immovable property (currently or within the past 60 months), you do not have to notify us when you sell them.

If you purchase these types of shares from a non-resident who did not get a Certificate of Compliance, you do not have to withhold a portion of the purchase price.

Determining the value of your property

To prove that your property does not get most of its value from Canadian real or immovable property, you must obtain a written declaration:

  • For corporate shares: Obtain a declaration from the corporation.
  • For a trust interest or trust unit: Obtain a declaration from the trust.

This declaration must certify that the property's value does not primarily come from Canadian real or immovable property, and has not for the past 60 months.

For more information on purchaser's liability, see Information Circular IC72-17, Procedures Concerning the Disposition of Taxable Canadian Property by Non-Residents of Canada - Section 116.
