Notifying the CRA about property sales

When you are a non-resident vendor, you must notify the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) when you sell (dispose of) or plan to sell Canadian property. You must do this either before you sell the property or within 10 days after the sale.

We will issue you a certificate of compliance when we receive either:

  • an amount to cover the tax on your gain
  • appropriate security for the tax

Choose the correct form for your situation and send it to us with your payment or acceptable security to cover the tax you owe. Missing documents will delay your certificate.

  • General property: Use Form T2062, Request for Certificate of Compliance.
  • Resource, timber, or depreciable property: Use Form T2062A. You must also complete Form T2062ASCH1.
  • Life insurance policy: Your insurer will submit Form T2062B and Form T2062BSCH1 on your behalf.

What we will send you

  • Proposed sales: If you notify us about a proposed sale and provide payment or security, we will issue Form T2064, Certificate - Proposed Disposition of Property by a Non-Resident of Canada.
  • Actual sales: If you notify us about an actual sale and provide payment or security, we will issue Form T2068, Certificate - The Disposition of Property by a Non-Resident of Canada.

If the facts or amounts change after you receive a proposed certificate, you must send us an updated form. You must also provide additional payment or security to cover any tax increase. We will then issue Form T2068.

When you do not need to notify us

You do not need to notify us if you sold:

  • property that did not get most of its value from Canadian real or immovable property (for sales after March 5, 2010).
  • corporate shares that did not get most of their value from Canadian real or immovable property at any time in the past 60 months.
  • property that is fully treaty-protected.

Buying treaty-protected property

If you purchase treaty-protected property from a non-resident, special rules apply for Form T2062C, Notification of an Acquisition from a Non-Resident Vendor of Treaty-Protected Property.

  • If you are related to the vendor: You must send us Form T2062C within 30 days of buying the property. If you file late, your notification is invalid, and the vendor must notify us instead.
  • If you are not related to the vendor: You do not have to notify us. However, you can choose to file Form T2062C within 30 days to help reduce your liability as a purchaser. We will generally not hold you liable if you file on time and made a reasonable effort to ensure the property was treaty-protected.

We will only contact you about Form T2062C if you file it late.

How to send your forms

Online

Submit your forms using the "Submit Documents" feature in:

  • My Account for Individuals
  • Represent a Client
  • My Business Account

By mail

Send your forms to the Centre of Expertise (CoE) for the region where the property is located:

  • Real property (land or buildings): Use the property's legal or municipal address.
  • Business shares or assets: Use the corporation's head office address. (You may need to ask the corporation for this).
  • Estate or trust capital interest: Use the trustee's location.

If we ask you for more supporting documents, please include a cover page so we can route it correctly. Include:

  • the name of the person who requested the information
  • their CoE or tax services office
  • your case/file number or your tax identification number (SIN, ITN, or BN)
