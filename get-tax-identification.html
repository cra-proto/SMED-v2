What you need to get tax identification

Individual Tax Number (ITN)

Individual Tax Number (ITN) applications should be submitted separately from your disposition request to avoid delays. To apply, follow the instructions found at Applying for an Individual Tax Number.

On this page:

  • Individuals
  • Corporations
  • Trusts
  • Taxation numbers for treaty-protected property

Individuals

If you are a non-resident individual selling Canadian property, you must have a Canadian taxation number. Use one of the following:

  • Social Insurance Number (SIN) if you previously lived in Canada.
  • Temporary Tax Number (TTN) or Individual Tax Number (ITN) if you have filed a return before but do not have a SIN.

If you do not have any of these numbers, complete Form T1261, Application for a Canada Revenue Agency Individual Tax Number (ITN) for Non-Residents. Send this form to us separately before you sell your property to avoid delays.

Always include your SIN, TTN, or ITN on all payments and letters you send to us. This helps us make sure we apply your information to the correct account.

Corporations

You must have a Canadian Business Number (BN). You can get a BN by:

  • using Business Registration Online (BRO).
  • contacting the Business Windows Section at a designated tax services office.

For more information, see How to register for a business number or CRA program accounts.

Trusts

If your trust has filed a Canadian return before, it will already have a Trust account number.

If the trust has not filed a return, we will give you a special account number when you file your notification. Use this number on all your payments.

Taxation numbers for treaty-protected property

You do not need a taxation number to notify us when you acquire treaty-protected property from a non-resident.

However, if we have already issued you a SIN, TTN, ITN, BN, or Trust number, please include it on Form T2062C, Notification of an Acquisition from a Non-Resident Vendor of Treaty-Protected Property.
